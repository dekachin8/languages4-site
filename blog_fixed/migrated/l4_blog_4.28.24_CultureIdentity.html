Breathing Life into Language: The Heartbeat of Culture in Curriculum

It's because languages are more than just words; they're the essence of who we are, our stories, our ancestors, and the lands that shaped us. James A. Banks, a pioneer in multicultural education, eloquently expressed this notion:

"Most social scientists today view culture as consisting primarily of the symbolic, ideational, and intangible aspects of human societies... It is the values, symbols, interpretations, and perspectives that distinguish one people from another in modernized societies; it is not material objects and other tangible aspects of human societies." â€” Banks, J.A., Banks, & McGee, C.
